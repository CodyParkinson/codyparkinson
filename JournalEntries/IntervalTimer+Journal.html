3/30/2023
I am sick of not having an app that I can set multiple timers in. You would think that there would be a bunch of apps that include this feature. I want an app that can do two things: 1. Be able to set multiple, simultaneous timers. 2. Be able to start a new timer once a timer has ended.

For 1, did you know that the standard clock app on iPhone doesn’t have the feature to set multiple timers? What a dumb thing to not include. There are many times, when you are cooking for example, that you have multiple timers going. I like to wander around while I wait for things to finish, so I much prefer having a timer on my phone than on the oven. Plus, the oven only has one timer, so if I have two things baking, what am I to do?

For 2, I like to do circuit workouts. I would like a timer to restart once it has finished. In this, I also want there to be a cool down timer. For instance, I want a five minute timer to go 3 times with a one minute timer between all of them. I also want it to alert when there is 10 seconds left on each timer (or some other time left).

Now, it isn’t that these apps don't exist, because they do. But, I cannot find one that has both of these features and is free. I am so cheap that I am going to learn how to code Swift, just to make this app “free”. I know I could just spend like $3 and get an app for both of these, but that isn’t fun.

This Journal is just a diary of everything that it took to go from zero Swift experience to an app on the App Store. I just got XCode downloaded, and I am watching the Stanford Lectures to try and teach myself the language. Today I finished the first two lectures, and I will be moving on to the assignments and readings in the next couple days. I figure if I can do the lectures almost like they are taught at Stanford, I can get it done within 5 or so weeks. Then I figure I can write the app in 2 weeks. Give myself an extra week and another extra week to get Apple to approve the app. This puts the final goal at nine weeks away or June 1st.

5/18/2023
I am so excited by the amount of progress that I have made in the last few days! I think I finally really figured out the hype of ChatGPT. The trick really is to learn how to ask very specific questions. I now feel like I have enough knowledge of Swift to really leverage AI. I have now played around a lot with ChatGPT, but I cannot get it to write very “good” code, at least not code that works within my app. But, what it does do is build really good templates to work from. This is my advice to others just learning a language, let ChatGPT do all the really dumb stuff.

For example, I wanted to build a ‘Tips’ page. I asked chatGPT to write me a generic SwiftUI help page, and boom, instantly done. What would have taken me probably a few hours to Google and figure out was done in just a few seconds. I could then look over the code and figure it out from there. Any errors that popped up are an easy search away, heck if you just ask ChatGPT, it can sometimes figure out its own mistake!

It may sound like cheating, but I feel like I am picking up the language very fast. I can look at what was written and work backwards through it. If you have no knowledge of the language, it will be very difficult to work with what ChatGPT spits out. It has made me even more excited to go and learn new topics. I’ll ask for an idea on how to do something, and it will give me multiple ways of trying the problem. This has led me down lots of rabbit holes, and now I feel like I have really practical Swift knowledge.

I hate when I’m trying to learn something and I can’t quite see if I am going down the right path. You can learn so much lingo and tricks, but most of the time, you will never use it. It’s more just ‘gee-wiz’ info. But, by learning by building, and with the aid of ChatGPT, you can very easily see the practicality, because it is part of the foundation of how the app actually functions.

Well that’s my rant about my process, here’s some updates on the app itself! Firstly, well everything is pretty much different, new looks, new pages, everythings. I locked it to dark mode, because I don’t want to worry about writing and making the colors look good for light mode. Everyone should be using dark mode anyway. My original interval code is under “Quick Setup”. This is for those who just want a simple timer that goes: Time, Rest, Time, Rest, etc.

I added an interval builder. Here the user can create any kind of interval timer they want!

Next is the selection screen. Simply tap to see the details of the created Interval and press start! Once the timer starts, it looks just like my example above, but the background dynamically changes to the user’s color input. Also, it displays the next timer!

And, that’s about it! I have a list of things to edit, and I am still working on usability. Give me a couple more days, and I think I’ll be shipping it out to the Apple Store!
